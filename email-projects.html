Email Promotions

Single-page email tests promoting new posts, features, jobs, and tools to the user base.

Summary

Many Jobcase members engage in the platform primarily through email, making it an essential method of communication for reaching the user base to inform them of updates to the community and keep the in the loop about releases, jobs, and more.

Before transitioning to a role that focused on the experience of the web and app products, I worked to design and code responsive email experiences in a range of creative styles to hone in on the messaging and design that most resonated with the recipients.

Examples of Emails

A range of email designs with different messaging and in different styles that were sent to Jobcase users.

Fair Wage Calculator

Promotion of a tool that allows a user to enter information about their work history, education history, and location to get an estimate of their wage.

Trending Posts

An email promoting a conversation within the Jobcase social platform that was relevant to the interest of the recipient.

Following

Highlighting a feature meant to strengthen connections between users through following, providing value propositions and suggestions of users to  follow.

Job Alerts

A design alerting users of jobs in their area that the algorithm has determined might be a strong fit based on their skills and search history.

JobBot

An attempt to put a face to the job-matching algorithm using a cute robot.

Background

Overview

Desktop and mobile email designs to be sent to millions of users in order to get them to engage with the platform by searching for jobs, trying out a new tool, or contributing content to the community.

Requirements

The emails must be built to the highest standards of accessibility and use best practices. Because of a range of device types and screen sizes, QA was essential and utilized Litmus as well as in-house devices.

Role

Each of the emails were designed in a prototyping tool then coded with HTML and CSS by myself, to be reviewed by a team lead before being handed off to analysts for testing.

Turnaround

The typical email was delivered in 2-3 days, as rapid testing was essential to the marketing and outreach goals. This was achieved through a library of standardized components and a close relationship with a content strategist.
